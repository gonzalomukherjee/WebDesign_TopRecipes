What could be more delightful than entering your home on an early dark evening at 5pm and being greeted by the aroma of Texas-style chilli simmering in the slow cooker? 

This chili is sumptuously rich and uncomplicated, focusing solely on its main star: saucy, juicy, tender beef that effortlessly shreds with just a slight pull of the fork. 

My friend Alex introduced this recipe to me. It's always been a point of amusement between us because of the absurd 3/4 cup of chili powder. I've steered clear of using such a large amount of chili powder. Instead, I've drawn inspiration from that old, fondly-remembered recipe to create this version. The chili strikes a perfect balance with its savory, deep richness, yet it remains mild enough for my young ones to enjoy, particularly when topped with a dollop of sour cream, a favorite in their diet. 

And here's a final tip: I've transformed this chili into a casserole several times, crowning it with cornbread buttermilk biscuits. I plan to share this innovative twist on the internet very soon.

How To Make This Slow Cooker Texas Chili
1

Brown the beef.


Coat the beef with the salt; sear it in a hot skillet and brown the beef. Transfer to the slow cooker.

2

Cook the onion & garlic.


Brown them right in all that rendered fat from the beef. Flavor!

3

Add spices, tomatoes & chiles.

Get all your other stuff in the pan and let it cook for a few minutes. Again, flavor!

4

Move to the slow cooker.


At this point I like to move it over to the slow cooker to let it hang out on low for 6-7 hours.

5

Shred the beef.


I usually just pop the forks right in there and pull the meat apart gently to get big, tender chunks.

6

You’re Done!


Shred and serve with cheddar cheese, cornbread, sour cream, and green onions!
